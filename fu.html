Fujairah

Fujairah, located on the eastern coast of the United Arab Emirates, covers an area of about 1,450 square kilometers. It is an emirate of strategic importance for trade, thanks to its favorable location for maritime commerce.

Sheikh Hamad bin Mohammed Al Sharqi has been at the helm of Fujairah, guiding its development and growth. The emirate's economy is anchored in trade and industry, with a focus on economic diversification. The Port of Fujairah is one of the world's major oil bunkering and re-export centers, making it a critical hub for shipping and trade in the region.

Fujairah is celebrated for its stunning beaches along the Gulf of Oman, offering residents and visitors the opportunity to relax in a tranquil coastal setting. The emirate is also home to cultural attractions, including the Fujairah Fort, showcasing its historical significance.
